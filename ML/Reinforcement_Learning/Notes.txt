An agent learns by interacting with an environment and receiving rewards or penalties.

Think:

No labeled data (like supervised learning)
No pure pattern discovery (like unsupervised)
Instead → trial and error learning

Key Components of Reinforcement Learning:
Agent: The learner or decision-maker.
Environment: The world or context the agent interacts with.
Policy (
): The strategy the agent uses to determine the next action based on the current state.
Reward Signal: Feedback from the environment evaluating the last action (positive or negative).
Value Function: The long-term desirability of a state, considering future rewards, not just immediate ones.
